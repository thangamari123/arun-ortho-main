What We Treat

Comprehensive Fracture Management

Trauma and fractures require prompt, expert management to prevent complications and ensure optimal healing. At Arun Ortho, our emergency team is available around the clock to evaluate and stabilize all types of orthopaedic injuries, from simple forearm fractures to complex pelvic trauma.

We use a systematic ATLS (Advanced Trauma Life Support) approach for poly-trauma patients, collaborating with emergency physicians and other specialists. Our modern OT is equipped for both emergency and planned fracture surgeries including ORIF and intramedullary nailing.

Fracture Types We Treat

??

Simple & Compound Fractures

All closed and open fractures from the fingertips to the femur managed with appropriate conservative or surgical methods.

??

Open Fractures

Fractures with skin violation requiring emergency wound debridement, stabilization, and infection prevention.

??

Pelvic & Acetabular Fractures

Complex high-energy pelvic ring injuries requiring specialized fixation with external or internal techniques.

??

Spine Fractures

Vertebral compression fractures and unstable spine injuries requiring immobilization or surgical stabilization.

??

Poly-trauma

Multiple simultaneous injuries requiring prioritized, staged surgical management and intensive monitoring.

??

Non-union & Malunion

Failed or improperly healed fractures requiring corrective osteotomy, bone grafting, or re-fixation.

Treatment

Our Treatment Approach

01

Emergency Stabilization

Immediate assessment, splinting, pain control, and neurovascular status monitoring on arrival.

02

Imaging & Assessment

Emergency X-rays, CT scan, and clinical evaluation to define fracture pattern and plan management.

03

Surgical Fixation

ORIF (open reduction internal fixation), intramedullary nailing, or external fixation based on fracture type.

04

Post-operative Care

Wound management, DVT prophylaxis, early mobilization, and nutritional support for healing.

05

Rehabilitation

Progressive weight-bearing, physiotherapy, and radiological monitoring until full bone union and functional recovery.
