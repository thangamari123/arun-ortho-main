What We Treat

Understanding Neck & Back Pain

Neck and back pain are among the most common musculoskeletal complaints. At Arun Ortho, we provide a comprehensive evaluation of the entire spine — from the cervical (neck) region through the thoracic and lumbar (lower back) areas — using advanced imaging and clinical assessment.

Our approach combines non-surgical management with surgical expertise when required, always tailoring the treatment plan to the individual patient's condition, age, and lifestyle.

Conditions

What We Diagnose & Treat

Cervical Disc Herniation

Bulging or ruptured discs in the neck causing arm pain, numbness, or weakness.

Lumbar Disc Disease

Degenerated or herniated discs in the lower back leading to sciatica and leg pain.

Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal causing nerve compression, pain, and weakness.

Spondylolisthesis

Forward slipping of a vertebra causing instability, back pain, and nerve symptoms.

Scoliosis & Kyphosis

Abnormal spinal curvatures that may require bracing or surgical correction.

Muscle Spasms & Strain

Acute and chronic muscular conditions of the cervical and lumbar spine.

Treatment

Our Treatment Approach

01

Comprehensive Diagnosis

Physical exam, X-ray, MRI, CT scan to precisely identify the cause of your pain.

02

Conservative Management

Physiotherapy, pain medications, injections (epidural, nerve blocks), and activity modification.

03

Interventional Procedures

Minimally invasive procedures such as nucleoplasty, PLDD, or radiofrequency ablation when conservative care is insufficient.

04

Surgical Intervention

Microdiscectomy, laminectomy, spinal fusion, or disc replacement for severe or refractory cases.

05

Rehabilitation & Follow-up

Structured physiotherapy, lifestyle guidance, and regular monitoring for optimal long-term outcomes.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my back pain requires surgery?
Most back pain resolves with conservative care. Surgery is typically considered only if you have progressive nerve damage, weakness, or pain that doesn't improve after weeks of non-surgical treatments.
What is the recovery time for a slipped disc?
With conservative treatment like physiotherapy and medication, a slipped disc can improve within 4 to 6 weeks. If surgery is required, recovery may take a few months.
Are spine injections safe?
Yes, epidural steroid injections and nerve blocks are very safe when performed by experienced specialists. They provide significant relief by directly targeting inflammation around the nerves.
