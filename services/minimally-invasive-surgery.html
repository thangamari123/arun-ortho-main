What We Treat

The Advantage of Minimally Invasive Surgery

Minimally invasive orthopaedic surgery uses small incisions (typically less than 1 cm) and specialized instruments or cameras to perform complex procedures that previously required large open incisions. This approach significantly reduces muscle damage, blood loss, scarring, and post-operative pain.

Dr. Arun D. is highly trained in arthroscopic and percutaneous techniques across all major joints. Our state-of-the-art operating theatre is equipped with high-definition arthroscopic systems and computer navigation to ensure precision and safety.

Procedures We Perform

Arthroscopic Procedures

Diagnostic and therapeutic arthroscopy of knee, shoulder, hip, elbow, and ankle joints.

Percutaneous Fracture Fixation

Minimally invasive fracture fixation using small incisions and intramedullary nails or percutaneous screws.

Endoscopic Spine Surgery

Endoscopic discectomy and decompression for herniated discs with minimal muscle disruption.

Micro-Discectomy

Microsurgical removal of herniated disc material causing sciatica with excellent outcomes.

Computer-Navigated Replacement

Computer-assisted joint replacement for greater implant alignment accuracy and longevity.

Arthroscopic Rotator Cuff Repair

Complete reconstruction of torn rotator cuff tendons through small shoulder incisions.

Treatment

Our Treatment Approach

01

Pre-surgical Assessment

Complete clinical evaluation, blood work, anaesthesia consultation, and high-resolution imaging.

02

Anaesthesia Planning

Choice of regional (spinal/epidural) or general anaesthesia tailored to the procedure and patient.

03

Keyhole Procedure

Procedure performed through 2–3 small incisions using camera guidance and specialized instruments.

04

Day-Care or Short Stay Recovery

Most procedures allow discharge within 24–48 hours with oral pain medications and physiotherapy instructions.

05

Outpatient Physiotherapy

Structured rehabilitation program with progress milestones to achieve full functional recovery.
